# Performance of Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) ⚛️
This repository contains the results and code from an IT project focused on the performance of post-quantum cryptography. The project was conducted during the Summer Semester 2025 and Winter Semester 2025/2026.

***

## Project Overview
**Post-quantum cryptography (PQC)**, also known as quantum-safe cryptography, is designed to be secure against attacks from both classical and future quantum computers. A number of PQC algorithms have been proposed, and the first standards and open-source implementations are now in development.

This project involved **experimenting with existing PQC implementations to measure their performance** and assess their practical applicability in real-world use cases. Our goal was to understand the computational overhead and feasibility of integrating these new cryptographic methods.

***

## Getting Started
All project results and analyses are presented in a series of **Jupyter notebooks**. To begin, simply open the `overview.ipynb` notebook. This notebook will guide you through the project, providing an overview of the methodology and linking to the other detailed notebooks that contain the specific results and data.
